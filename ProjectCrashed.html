CRASHED

Made with Unity3D

My role in the team

After we had settled on an idea, and had it approved by the teachers we could start development. The first two weeks of the project was spent prototyping, where my role was to prototype: Enemy Spawning, movement, attacks and death. Camera movement and tracking. Player controls, death/respawning and weapon use. I was also involved in writing the Tech Document and setting up Perforce for the team.

After we had made a prototype we were satisfied with, we moved on to production. The 2nd sprint started and I was tasked with finalizing the camera behavior, implementing the enemy behavior and the playable characters mechanics.
During the 3nd sprint, I continued on the playable characters, adding a inventory system both visually and in code, making sure the equiped weapon showed up and behaved correctly. this consisted of being in constant communication with the artists, giving feedback when a model had to be split up into multiple parts or resized to keep it to scale.

The final sprint was dessignated to polishing the game, most my time was spent on finalizing the weapons behavior, communicating with the artists to get the sound and particle effets added into the game. Final polish on the enmies, and general gameplay adjustments we noticed as we tested the game.
